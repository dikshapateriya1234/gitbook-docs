# Right-Side Sub-Navigation

GitBook automatically builds the right-side panel from headings within your page.

## How It Works

When a page has multiple headings, GitBook lists them on the right side 
so readers can jump directly to any section without scrolling.

## Best Practices

Use double hash for major sections within a page. Use triple hash for 
sub-sections. Avoid going deeper than four hash marks. Aim for 4 to 8 
headings per page for clean right-side navigation.

## When It Does Not Appear

The right panel only shows if the page has more than one heading. 
Single-heading pages will not show right-side navigation. This is why 
it is important to break your content into clearly named sections.

## Example Heading Structure

A well-structured page might look like this in terms of headings:
Introduction, Prerequisites, Main Steps, Troubleshooting, and Summary. 
Each of these would appear as a clickable link in the right panel.

## Why This Matters

Right-side navigation is especially useful for long pages. It lets 
readers scan the full structure of a page at a glance and jump to 
exactly the section they need without reading everything above it.
